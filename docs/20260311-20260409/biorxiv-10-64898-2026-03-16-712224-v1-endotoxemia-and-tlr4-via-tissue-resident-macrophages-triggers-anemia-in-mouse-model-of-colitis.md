## 摘要
贫血是炎症性肠病（IBD）最严重且最常见的并发症之一，通常采用铁剂补充治疗，但疗效有限。肠道屏障功能受损是IBD的标志，会导致肠道细菌的内毒素易位进入血液。在我们之前的一项小鼠研究中，我们报道了内毒素通过重编程幼红细胞岛巨噬细胞（EBI Mφ）来抑制红细胞生成。在此，我们展示了IBD患者和急性结肠炎小鼠出现了与贫血相关的内毒素血症。IBD患者的内毒素血症与血液红细胞计数呈负相关。与此一致，饮用含有葡聚糖硫酸钠（DSS）的水引起的急性结肠炎小鼠表现出内毒素血症及贫血，其特征为红细胞计数、血红蛋白含量和红细胞压积降低，以及骨髓红细胞生成减少（部分由髓外红细胞生成增加所补偿）。由于内毒素受体TLR4在骨髓中的CD169+肠道驻留巨噬细胞和红细胞岛巨噬细胞中表达，我们测试了由这些CD169+巨噬细胞表达的TLR4介导炎症性结肠炎和贫血的假设。事实上，在CD169+组织驻留巨噬细胞中特异性条件性缺失Tlr4基因的小鼠免受DSS诱导的贫血和结肠炎的影响。此外，用TLR4抑制剂C34治疗DSS小鼠减轻了炎症和贫血。这些结果表明，从发炎肠道渗漏的内毒素可能在IBD及其相关贫血中起关键作用，而针对TLR4的药物可能预防IBD相关贫血。关键点：1. IBD患者和急性结肠炎小鼠患有贫血，且内毒素血症和炎症增加。2. IBD患者的内毒素血症与血液红细胞计数呈负相关。3. 在CD169+组织驻留巨噬细胞中特异性条件性缺失内毒素受体基因Tlr4，或施用合成TLR4抑制剂，可显著减轻小鼠结肠炎诱导的贫血。

## Abstract
Anemia is one of the most debilitating and frequent complications of inflammatory bowel diseases (IBD) and is often treated with iron supplementation, which has limited efficacy. Damaged intestinal barrier function is a hallmark of IBD and causes the translocation of endotoxins from gut bacteria into the bloodstream. In a previous study in mice, we reported that endotoxin suppresses erythropoiesis by reprogramming erythroblastic island macrophages (EBI M{varphi}). Here, we show that IBD patients and mice with acute colitis developed endotoxemia associated with anemia. Endotoxemia in IBD patients was negatively correlated with blood erythrocyte counts. In line with this, mice with acute colitis caused by drinking water containing dextrin sodium sulphate (DSS) had endotoxemia together with anemia characterized by reduced red blood cell counts, hemoglobin content and hematocrit., and reduced medullary erythropoiesis which was in part compensated by increased extramedullary erythropoiesis. As the endotoxin receptor TLR4 is expressed by CD169+ gut-resident macrophages and erythroid island macrophages in the bone marrow, we tested the hypothesis that TLR4 expressed by these CD169+ macrophages mediate both inflammatory colitis and anemia. Indeed, mice with conditional deletion of the Tlr4 gene specifically in CD169+ tissue-resident macrophages were protected from DSS-induced anemia and colitis. In addition, treatment of DSS mice with the TLR4 inhibitor C34 abated inflammation and anemia. These results suggest that endotoxins leaking from the inflamed gut may play a crucial role in IBD and associated anemia and that drugs targeting TLR4 may protect against IBD-associated anemia.
