## 摘要
背景：炎症性肠病（IBD）是一种以肠道微生物失调和免疫调节异常为特征的慢性炎症性疾病。虽然微生物组的组成变化已得到深入研究，但微生物影响宿主信号传导的功能机制仍不清楚。目的：本研究旨在调查IBD中的微生物-宿主分子模拟，并阐明其通过新提出的微生物信号识别与神经模拟（SRNM）轴在调节免疫和神经通路中的作用。方法：使用定制的分子模拟计算机模拟流水线（MMIP）分析了来自IBD患者和健康对照者的鸟枪法宏基因组数据集。对读取序列进行组装、注释，并进行蛋白质同源性映射、基因本体（GO）富集、PFAM结构域分析和分类学分析，以识别模拟人类功能通路的微生物蛋白质。结果：与健康对照组相比，IBD相关微生物组表现出显著更高的功能复杂性和真核生物样蛋白质富集。模拟宿主通路的微生物蛋白质在IBD中显著富集，这些通路涉及神经元突起发育、信号识别颗粒（SRP）介导的蛋白质靶向、免疫信号传导和应激反应。关键的类人靶点包括TRPV1、CAMK2D、SNCA、MTCP1、TCL1B和PEAK3。PFAM分析显示激酶结构域、锌指基序、锚蛋白重复序列和ABC转运蛋白过度表达。这些特征主要由IBD富集的分类群贡献，如γ-变形菌纲（Gammaproteobacteria）、梭杆菌门（Fusobacteria）和β-变形菌纲（Betaproteobacteria）。结论：本研究确定了IBD中以前未被认识的SRNM轴，揭示了微生物分子模拟如何影响神经免疫信号传导和疾病发病机制，并突出了基于微生物组的治疗干预的潜在靶点。

## Abstract
BackgroundInflammatory bowel disease (IBD) is a chronic inflammatory disorder characterized by gut microbial dysbiosis and immune dysregulation. While compositional changes in the microbiome are well studied, the functional mechanisms through which microbes influence host signalling remain poorly understood.

PurposeThis study aimed to investigate microbial-host molecular mimicry in IBD and to elucidate its role in modulating immune and neuronal pathways through a newly proposed Microbial Signal Recognition and Neuronal Mimicry (SRNM) axis.

MethodsShotgun metagenomic datasets from IBD patients and healthy controls were analyzed using a custom Molecular Mimicry In Silico Pipeline (MMIP). Reads were assembled, annotated, and subjected to protein homology mapping, Gene Ontology enrichment, PFAM domain analysis, and taxonomic profiling to identify microbial proteins mimicking human functional pathways.

ResultsIBD-associated microbiomes exhibited significantly higher functional complexity and enrichment of eukaryote-like proteins compared to healthy controls. Microbial proteins mimicking host pathways involved in neuron projection development, signal recognition particle (SRP)-mediated protein targeting, immune signaling, and stress responses were markedly enriched in IBD. Key human-like targets included TRPV1, CAMK2D, SNCA, MTCP1, TCL1B, and PEAK3. PFAM analysis revealed overrepresentation of kinase domains, zinc-finger motifs, ankyrin repeats, and ABC transporters. These signatures were predominantly contributed by IBD-enriched taxa such as Gammaproteobacteria, Fusobacteria, and Betaproteobacteria.

ConclusionThis study identifies a previously unrecognized SRNM axis in IBD, revealing how microbial molecular mimicry may influence neuroimmune signaling and disease pathogenesis, and highlight potential targets for microbiome-based therapeutic intervention.
