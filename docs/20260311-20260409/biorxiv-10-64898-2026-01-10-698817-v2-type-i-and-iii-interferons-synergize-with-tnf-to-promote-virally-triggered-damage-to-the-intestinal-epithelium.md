## 摘要
长期免疫激活导致的肠上皮细胞过度死亡与克罗恩病等肠道疾病相关。携带克罗恩病易感基因 Atg16l1 突变的小鼠易发生与肠上皮细胞 (IEC) 死亡相关的炎症，包括由鼠诺如病毒 (MNV) 感染触发的抗菌潘氏细胞 (Paneth cells) 丢失。在本文中，我们证明 MNV 诱发的下游肠道疾病依赖于 IEC 中的 IFN-α/β 和 IFN-λ 信号传导。在小鼠类器官中，干扰素 (IFNs) 与 TNF 协同诱导 RIPK1 依赖性细胞死亡，且这种作用因 ATG16L1 缺失而增强。我们进一步表明，携带 ATG16L1 风险等位基因的人类肠道类器官对 TNF 和 IFN 的共同刺激以及重症 COVID-19 患者的血清表现出更高的敏感性。我们的研究结果揭示，包括干扰素在内的病毒诱导细胞因子会加剧具有遗传易感性宿主的上皮脆弱性。

## Abstract
Excessive cell death in the epithelium due to prolonged immune activation is associated with intestinal diseases such as Crohns disease. Mice with mutations in the Crohns disease susceptibility gene Atg16l1 are susceptible to inflammation associated with intestinal epithelial cell (IEC) death, including loss of antimicrobial Paneth cells triggered by infection with murine norovirus (MNV). Here, we show that intestinal disease downstream of MNV depends on IFN-/{beta} and IFN-{lambda} signaling in IECs. In mouse organoids, IFNs synergize with TNF to induce RIPK1-dependent cell death, amplified by ATG16L1 deficiency. We further show that human intestinal organoids harboring the ATG16L1 risk allele exhibit heightened sensitivity to TNF and IFN co-stimulation and to serum from severe COVID-19 patients. Our findings reveal that virally triggered cytokines including IFNs exacerbate epithelial vulnerability in genetically predisposed hosts.
