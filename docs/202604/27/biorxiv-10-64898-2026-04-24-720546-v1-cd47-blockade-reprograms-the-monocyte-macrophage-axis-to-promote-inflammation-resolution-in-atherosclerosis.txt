Abstract 1

Atherosclerosis is a chronic inflammatory disease and  a leading cause of  cardiovascular 2

mortality  worldwide. Disease progression is closely linked to defective efferocytosis,  the 3

impaired clearance  of apoptotic cells , which drives necrotic core expansion  and 4

perpetuates arterial  inflammation.  Targeting the CD47 -SIRPα  innate immune checkpoint ,5

a dominant “don’t -eat -me” signal, limits atherosclerosis in preclinical models and 6

retrospective human  studies.  However, how  pro -efferocytic intervention  reshape s the 7

immune landscape of  established  atherosclerotic lesions  remains  incompletely 8

understood. 9

Here, using single -cell transcriptomics, monocyte fate mapping, and functional analyses 10 

across complementary preventive and interventive murine  atherosclerosis model s, we 11 

demonstrate that  CD47  blockade fundamentally  reprograms  the myeloid landscape of 12 

established lesions. Anti -CD47  therapy  selectively  suppresses inflammatory Ly6C hi 13 

monocyte recruitment and reduces  local  macrophage proliferation without  altering  overall 14 

plaque macrophage burden , indicating a qualitative rather th an  quantitative remodeling 15 

of the infiltrate . Concurrently , therapy enriches  macrophage  subsets bearing pro -16 

efferocytic  and macrophage survival -associated transcriptional programs, restoring 17 

defective  apoptotic cell clearance in situ.  Cross -species integration with  an independent 18 

human coronary artery single -cell dataset identifies a conserved T REM 2hi  macrophage 19 

population that  natively harbors  the efferocytosis machinery reactivated by therapy in 20 

mice. 21 

Together, these findings  demonstrate  that innate immune checkpoint inhibition  by CD47 22 

blo ckade  drives  a coordinated reprogramming of monocyte –macrophage dynamics ,23

Introduction 1

Atherosclerosis is a lipoprotein -driven chronic inflammatory disease. While lipid -2

lowering  therapies , such as statins  and  PCSK9 inhibitors 1, ha ve  improved clinical 3

outcomes, residual cardiovascular  risk  remains, largely driven  by  unresolved arterial 4

inflammation 2,3 . Although  atherosclerotic plaques harbor a  highly  complex immune 5

microenvironment  involving both adaptive (e.g.,  T and B  cells ) and innate  immune 6

populations 4-6, plaque -associated  macrophages  occupy a central position in  driving 7

disease progression 7. Originating  primarily from  functionally distinct  circulating monocyte 8

subsets  (such as  pro -inflammatory Ly6C hi  and patrolling Ly6C lo  monocytes )8-10 , these 9

cells govern plaque composition.  Beyond  aberrant lipid uptake and processing, plaque 10 

macrophages exhibit impaired clearance of apoptotic cells, a process termed 11 

efferocytosis 11 -15 . Defective efferocytosis drives necrotic core  expansion,  destabilizes 12 

plaques,  and  perpetuates local  inflammation 11,16,17 . Therefore , restoring innate immune 13 

homeostasis and macrophage function  represent s a prime  therapeutic  target. 14 

A key driver of this impaired clearance is the  upregulation of Cluster of 15 

Differentiation 47 (CD47), a “don’t -eat -me” signal  that interacts with  Signal -Regulatory 16 

Protein alpha (SIRPα) on myeloid cells  to inhibit phagocytosis 18 -20 . Targeting  this innate 17 

immune checkpoint has emerged as a promising strategy: anti -CD47 antibodies 18 

reactivate efferocytosis , reduce lesion burden , and  have been associated  with reduced 19 

vascular inflammation in both  preclinical  and retrospective human stud ies 20 -25 . Notably, 20 

while landmark clinical trials like  CANTOS  (targeting IL -1β via canakinumab ) validated 21 

the inflammatory hypothesis of atherosclerosis, they also highlighted the infectious  risks 22

not merely suppress inflammation but actively induce resolution and tissue repair. 2

While  the CD47 -SIRPα axis represents the only pro -efferocytic strategy  to have 3

transitioned to clinical testing  in  atherosclerotic  cardiovascular  disease 27 , a critical 4

knowledge gap remains . Despite evidence that CD47 blockade enhances efferocytosis, 5

it is unknown whether this therapy  solely augment s immediate phagocytic activity or 6

whether  it fundamentally reprogram s the  underlying  inflammatory monocyte –7

macrophage axis . The precise mechanisms by which this checkpoint blockade alters the 8

local  immune landscape remain undefined. 9

Here, we combine single -cell transcriptomics with functional analyses in murine 10 

atherosclerosis and cross -species validation in human coronary plaques to define how 11 

pro -efferocytic therapy remodels the innate immune compartment. We demonstrate that 12 

CD47 blockade reshapes the immune landscape toward a pro -resolution state by 13 

modulating the monocyte -macrophage  axis , reactivating a conserved efferocytosis -14 

competent macrophage program, and altering inflammatory recruitment and local 15 

proliferation.  Furthermore , we identify a homologous macrophage population in human 16 

plaques that  share s this machinery, supporting the translational relevance of macrophage 17 

reprogramming as a therapeutic mechanism of anti -CD47 therapy. Together, these 18 

findings  advance our mechanistic understanding of pro -efferocytic therapy and  provide  a19 

translational  framework  to  inform  the clinical  development  of  pro -efferocytic treatments. 20

Statistical Analysis 3

Statistical analyses were performed using GraphPad Prism 10 (GraphPad Inc.).  Data  and 4

error bars  present  mean  ± SD  for parametric and median  ± IQR for non -parametric 5

results . Normality of data was determined by performing a Shapiro –Wilk normality test (α 6

= 0.05). Normally distributed data were analy zed using an unpaired Student’s  t-test (two -7

tailed)  or  one -way analysis of variance  (ANOVA)  with Tukey’s multiple comparisons test 8

or two -way ANOVA with  Geisser -Greenhouse correction . For s amples that had unequal 9

variances (determined by F -test), an unpaired Welch’s t -test (two -tailed) was used. If the 10 

data set did not pass the normality test (p -value  < 0.05), the statistical significance was 11 

determined by Mann Whitney U test (two -tailed)  or Kruskal -Wallis test , followed by Dunn’s 12 

multiple comparisons test for more than two groups . Exact p -values are indicated.  P13 

values  for the diff erentially expressed genes  were adjusted  for multiple  testing using the 14 

Benjamin -Hochberg  FDR.  All data behind the statistical analysis and all p values are 15 

provided in Source Data. 16

Anti -CD47  Treatment  Reprograms the  Aortic  Myeloid  Landscape .2

Blockade of the CD47 -SIRPα axis promotes atherosclerotic plaque regression and 3

resolves vascular inflammation across murine models, large animals, and human 4

cohorts 20 -23,29 . However, the cellular mechanisms driving these therapeutic effects remain 5

incompletely understood. To map the immunomodulatory landscape of CD47 blockade in 6

a highly translationally relevant settin g, Ldlr -/- mice were fed a  high -fat, high -sucrose 7

Western  Diet  for 12 weeks 30,31 . We then  performed single -cell RNA -sequencing (scRNA -8

seq) on viable CD45 + aortic leukocytes from  Ldlr -/- mice in a prevention setting , treat ing 9

the mice  concurrent ly with an  anti -CD47  antibody  or  an  IgG isotype  control for 12 weeks 10 

(Figure 1A  and  Figure S1A). Of note, anti -CD47 treated animals did not show any 11 

differences in their body and heart weight but had an increased spleen weight as reported 12 

previously (Figure S1B –S1D) 20,24 . To confirm the induction of the pro -atherogenic 13 

metabolic  phenotype, circulating lipids and non -fasting glucose  were  compared  between 14 

the cohorts (Figure S1E). We integrated 2,854 high -quality cells  from our experimental 15 

cohorts  (1,237 IgG; 1,617 anti -CD47) with  1,315  reference  cells  from  a public ly available 16 

scRNA -seq  dataset of  Ldlr -/- mice  under  chow  diet  (CD , n = 381 ) and  Western Diet  (WD, 17 

n =  934) 4, yielding a consolidated atlas of 4,169 cells  after batch correction  (Figure 1B ;18 

Figure S1F  and S1G ). Unsupervised clustering based on canonical marker genes (e.g., 19 

Apo e, Cd3e, Cd209a, Trem2)  revealed seven major immune cell lineages (Figure 1B and 20 

1C). While the IgG control group mirrored the atherogenic  WD  phenotype, anti -CD47 21 

treatment shifted  the  mononuclear phagocyte (MPC) and antigen -presenting cell (APC) 22 

compositions toward a non -atherogenic baseline (Figure 1D). To resolve myeloid 23

Discussion 1

The present study demonstrates  that pro -efferocytic therapy reshapes 2

atherosclerotic inflammation  through  mechanisms that extend substantially beyond 3

apoptotic cell clearance and lesion regression.  Using a model of established 4

atherosclerosis in which CD47 blockade did not alter lesion size, w e show  that  anti -CD47 5

therapy  re models  the plaque  immune  microenvironment and  promotes  inflammation 6

resolution  by fundamentally reprogramming the monocyte -macrophage axis . Specifically, 7

CD47 blockade  selectively suppresses pro -inflammatory monocyte infiltration and 8

lesional macrophage proliferation while reactivating the efferocytosis machinery within 9

the myeloid compartment, a transcriptional program we find to be conserved in human 10 

coronary  atherosclerosis . Innate immune checkpoint inhibition targeting the CD47 -SIRPα 11 

axis is  under  clinical  investigation in  hematologic malignancies and solid tumors 46 , and 12 

has more recently been  evaluated  in  atherosclerotic cardiovascular disease. Despite 13 

preclinical  evidence supporting  plaque regression, the precise immune mechanisms 14 

underlying these therapeutic effects  have  remain ed  incompletely understood ; the present 15 

findings  begin to address this gap .16 

Recent single -cell analyses of atherosclerotic plaques have identified a specific 17 

subset of foamy macrophages characterized by elevated expression of  Trem2 4,5,47 . These 18 

TREM2 hi foamy macrophages exhibit a reduced pro -inflammatory capacity compared to 19 

other plaque subsets  and are essential for apoptotic cell clearance 5,47 . Indeed,  Trem2 -/-20 

macrophages display impaired efferocytosis, leading to necrotic core expansion  in 21 

hyperlipidemic mice 32 . In this context, we observed that CD47 blockade exerts stage -22 

dependent effects on this population.  During early atherogenesis, anti -CD47 therapy 23

Although  the  innate immune reprogramming  and inflammation resolution dynamics 3

described here were observed in a well -established  Western Diet  model of 4

atherosclerosis 30,32,33 , certain  limitations  warrant consideration . First,  our scRNA -seq 5

experiments were performed on pooled aortic leukocytes , yielding a single sequencing 6

library per experimental group,  a design consistent with published aortic single -cell 7

atlases 4,34  and necessitated by the low immune cell yield from individual aortas . However, 8

this approach  precludes formal sta tistical replication at the sequencing level and limits 9

assessment of  inter -individual transcriptional variability . Critically, all major 10 

transcriptional ly  defined ob servations  were independently corroborated by flow 11 

cytometry, immunofluorescence, qPCR, and functional efferocytosis assays in fully 12 

replicated cohorts ; these orthogonal validations provide the primary statistical evidence 13 

supporting each claim . Multiplexed single -cell approaches  with individual -level 14 

sequencing replication  will be important  in future studies . Second,  while the  Western Diet -15 

fed  Ldlr -/- model  recapitulates  metabolic  atherosclero sis and has been extensively 16 

characterized  as  a type 2 diabetes -like state 31 , our  study  did not perform comprehensive 17 

metabolic phenotyping beyond  circulating lipid measurements  and non -fasting glucose .18 

Formal parameters of systemic insulin resistance , such as  fasting  insulin levels  and 19 

HOMA -IR , were  not  assessed and therefore , insulin resistance  was not  independently 20 

established  in our cohorts . Third , our  investigation  focused  primarily  on  the  innate immune 21 

compartment ; adaptive immune populations were not deeply profiled. Given the emerging 22 

role of T and B cells in modulating plaque  phenotype  and  inflammation  resolution 35,52,53 ,23

In conclusion, our data demonstrate that pro -efferocytic therapy  promot es 13 

inflammation  resolution in  established atherosclerosis  not only  through  reactivati on  of 14 

apoptotic cell  clearance , but  also  through  coordinated  reprogramming  of  the monocyte –15 

macrophage axis  with in a chronic  pro -atherogenic  metabolic environment . By 16 

suppressing Ly6C hi  monocyte  recruitment , reducing  local  macrophage proliferation, and 17 

enriching efferocytic  capacity  in plaque -associated phagocytes, CD47 blockade  is 18 

associated with  inflammation resolution while maintaining  lesional  macrophage stability ,19 

effects that may be of particular relevance in patients with established disease and 20 

residual vascular inflammation despite lipid -lowering therapy . These findings position 21 

reactivation of innate efferocytosis as a mechanistically distinct and translationally 22 

relevant strategy for  addressing  residual cardiovascular  risk .23
