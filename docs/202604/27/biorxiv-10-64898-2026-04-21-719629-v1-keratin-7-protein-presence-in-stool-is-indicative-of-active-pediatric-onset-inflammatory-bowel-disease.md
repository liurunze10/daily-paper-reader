## 摘要
背景：炎症性肠病（IBD）与肠上皮细胞的早期结构变化有关；然而，相关的分子改变仍未完全明确。最近发现细胞骨架蛋白角蛋白（K）7在IBD的结肠上皮中呈局灶性表达，而在健康结肠中则不存在。本研究探讨了K7作为儿童IBD无创粪便生物标志物的适用性。方法：在这项病例对照研究中，包括青少年IBD患者（n=27）和健康对照者（n=15），通过蛋白质组学、免疫测定和qPCR分别分析粪便裂解物，以确定K7蛋白和mRNA含量。此外，还测量了简单上皮角蛋白K8、K18、K19和K20的粪便mRNA水平。结果：粪便蛋白质组学分析在IBD样本中鉴定出局灶性K7和K19。此外，鉴定了23种差异丰度蛋白，其中18种在IBD中较高，基因本体（Gene Ontology）富集分析突出了免疫和炎症通路。K7特异性免疫测定在所有活动期IBD患者（包括溃疡性结肠炎和克罗恩病）中均检测到粪便K7蛋白，而在对照组和缓解期IBD患者中，K7接近或低于检测限（ROC曲线下面积=0.88，p<0.0001）。虽然KRT7 mRNA水平低于检测限，但与对照组相比，IBD样本中的KRT8和KRT18转录本升高（p<0.05）。结论：IBD患者粪便中K7蛋白升高，反映了肠道从头表达和上皮细胞脱落增加。粪便K7可能为IBD的诊断和监测提供一种新型、无创的标志物。

## Abstract
BackgroundInflammatory bowel disease (IBD) is associated with early structural changes in intestinal epithelial cells; however, the associated molecular alterations remain incompletely understood. The cytoskeletal protein keratin (K) 7 was recently found to be focally expressed in the colonic epithelium in IBD, while absent in the healthy colon. Here, we investigated the applicability of K7 as a noninvasive stool biomarker for pediatric IBD.

MethodsIn this case-control study including adolescent patients with IBD (n=27) and healthy controls (n=15), stool lysates were analyzed by proteomics, immunoassay and qPCR to determine K7 protein and mRNA content, respectively. Additionally, stool mRNA levels of the simple epithelial keratins, K8, K18, K19 and K20, were measured.

ResultsStool proteomic analysis identified focal K7 and K19 in IBD samples. Additionally, 23 differentially abundant proteins, of which 18 were higher in IBD, were identified and Gene Ontology enrichment analysis highlighted immune and inflammatory pathways. K7 specific immunoassay detected fecal K7 protein in all patients with active IBD, including both ulcerative colitis and Crohns disease, while K7 was near or below the detection limit in controls and IBD patients in remission (area under ROC curve=0.88, p<0.0001). While KRT7 mRNA levels were below the detection limit, KRT8 and KRT18 transcripts were elevated in IBD samples compared to controls (p<0.05).

ConclusionsK7 protein is elevated in IBD patient stool, reflecting intestinal de novo expression and increased epithelial cell exfoliation. Fecal K7 may provide a novel, noninvasive marker for IBD diagnosis and monitoring.
