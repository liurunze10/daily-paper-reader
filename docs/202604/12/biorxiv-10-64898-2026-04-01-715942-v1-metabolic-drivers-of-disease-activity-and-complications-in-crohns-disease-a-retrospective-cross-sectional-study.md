## 摘要
背景：本研究旨在探讨代谢综合征（MetS）各组分与克罗恩病（CD）患者两项关键临床结局之间的独立关系：以克罗恩病活动指数（CDAI）量化的疾病活动度以及并发症的发生。方法：这项回顾性横断面研究纳入了376名新诊断为克罗恩病的成年患者。采用多元线性回归分析代谢参数与CDAI评分之间的关联，并利用多因素逻辑回归评估其与并发症的联系。分析还基于临床CDAI切点进行。开发了预测列线图，并通过自助抽样法（bootstrap resampling）进行了内部验证。结果：多元线性回归显示，较高的CDAI评分与较低的BMI（B = -5.866, P < 0.001）、较低的高密度脂蛋白胆固醇（HDL-C）水平（B = -81.770, P < 0.001）、较高的甘油三酯（TG）（B = 15.618, P = 0.001）以及较低的红细胞沉降率（ESR）（B = -0.375, P = 0.03）独立相关。多因素逻辑回归确定低HDL-C（OR = 0.042, P < 0.001）、低BMI（OR = 0.915, P = 0.034）和高甘油三酯（OR = 1.792, P = 0.007）是并发症显著的独立危险因素。开发的列线图表现出强大的预测性能，CDAI模型的调整后R²为0.207，并发症模型的曲线下面积（AUC）为0.765。对于这两项预测任务，包含独立TG和HDL-C测量值的模型表现显著优于TG/HDL-C比值模型。结论：代谢紊乱与克罗恩病疾病严重程度的增加以及并发症发生风险的升高显著相关。核心提示：1. 这项双结局研究揭示了HDL-C和TG与CD炎症及并发症的不同联系，指向了不同的机制。2. 低HDL-C是CD并发症最强的独立预测因子，强调了其在胆固醇转运之外的保护作用。3. 独立的TG和HDL-C指标在预测中优于其比值，挑战了该比值的使用，并提示CD中存在独立的代谢途径。4. 低BMI与两种不良结局均独立相关，修正了“肥胖悖论”并突显了营养不良的关键作用。5. 一个经过验证的实用列线图（AUC=0.765）整合了HDL-C、TG和BMI，用于对并发症风险进行分层，辅助临床决策。

## Abstract
BackgroundThis study aims to examine the independent relationships between individual components of metabolic syndrome (MetS) and two key clinical outcomes in patients with Crohns disease (CD): disease activity, as quantified by the Crohns Disease Activity Index (CDAI), and the occurrence of complications.

MethodsThis retrospective cross-sectional study included 376 adults with newly diagnosed Crohns disease. Multiple linear regression was used to examine associations between metabolic parameters and CDAI scores, while multivariate logistic regression assessed links to complications. Analyses were also based on clinical CDAI cut-offs. Predictive nomograms were developed and internally validated via bootstrap resampling.

ResultsMultiple linear regression indicated that higher CDAI scores were independently associated with lower BMI (B = -5.866, P < 0.001), lower HDL-C levels (B = -81.770, P < 0.001), higher triglycerides (B = 15.618, P = 0.001), and lower ESR (B = -0.375, P = 0.03). Multivariate logistic regression established low HDL-C (OR = 0.042, P < 0.001), low BMI (OR = 0.915, P = 0.034), and high triglycerides (OR = 1.792, P = 0.007) as significant independent risk factors for complications. The developed nomograms demonstrated strong predictive performance, with an adjusted R2 of 0.207 for the CDAI model and an AUC of 0.765 for the complication model. For both predictive tasks, the model incorporating separate TG and HDL-C measurements significantly outperformed the TG/HDL-C ratio model.

ConclusionMetabolic disturbances demonstrate a significant association with increased disease severity and a higher risk of complication development in Crohns disease.
