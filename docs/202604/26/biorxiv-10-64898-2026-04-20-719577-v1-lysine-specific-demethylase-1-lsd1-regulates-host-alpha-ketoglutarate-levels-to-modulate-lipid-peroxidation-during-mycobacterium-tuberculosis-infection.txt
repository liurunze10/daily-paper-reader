ABSTRACT 13 

Mycobacterium tuberculosis (Mtb) subvert s host immune responses via modulation of 14 

host epigenome and metabolism. In this study, we underscore a role for the epigenetic 15 

modifier, Lysine Specific Demethylase 1 (LSD1), in regulating macrophage 16 

metabolism to support mycobacterial pathogenesi s. In ex vivo and in vivo infection 17 

models, LSD1 inhibition reduced mycobacterial CFU alleviat ing lung pathology. 18 

Metabolomic analysis of Mtb infected, LSD1 deficient macrophages revealed 19 

increase d levels of alpha -ketoglutarate (AKG) , a crucial TCA cycle metabolite via 20 

regulati ng genes implicated in glutamine breakdown . Moreover, e xogenous addition 21 

of AKG resulted in reduced oxidative stress and attenuat ed lipid peroxidation (LPO) 22 

with a consequent decrease in Mtb surviva l. Blocking glutamine breakdown in LSD1 23 

deficient macrophages failed to reduce LPO and promoted Mtb intracellular survival ,24 

highlighting the role of LSD1 -AKG axis in this immunomodulation . Dietary 25 

supplementation of AKG to Mtb infected mice improved lung pathology , limited Mtb 26 

dissemination and reduced the levels of oxidative Malondialdehyde adducts. 27 

Therefore, we highlight a host protective role of AKG during Mtb pathogenesis through 28 

suppression of lipid peroxidatio n and uncover an epigenetic -metabolic axis exploited 29 

by Mtb , thereby posit ing dietary supplementation of AKG as a potential therapeutic 30 

strategy against Tuberculosis .31

Given the current line of investigation, it becomes crucial to assess whether 167 

exogenous addition of AKG could be beneficial to host during Mtb infection. To this 168 

end , we carried out an ex vivo Mtb CFU enumeration and macrophage cell death 169 

assay upon AKG supplementation. Intracellular survival of Mtb in murine macrophages 170 

was decreased upon AKG supplementation (Figure 5A) and Mtb induced macrophage 171 

cell death was reduced upon AKG supplementation (Figure 5B ), hinting towards an 172 

overall host protective role of AKG during Mtb infection. 173 

During infections, AKG is generally implicated in reduc ing host stress and 174 

resol ving inflammation via its antioxidant activity 44 –46 . In addition to being a direct 175 

quencher of hydrogen peroxide, AKG promotes expression of anti -oxidant genes, 176 

promote synthesis of Glutathione and increases GPX4 levels, a key antioxidant 177 

enzyme implicated in reducing lipid peroxidation 24 . Indeed, we found a reduction in 178 

macrophage Reactive Oxygen Species ( ROS ) levels upon AKG supplementation 179 

during Mtb infection as measured by CellRox staining (Figure 5C, D). Excess ive 180 

oxidative stress in the cells lead to lipid peroxidation (LPO) which i s implicated in aiding 181 

Mtb dissemination and pathogenesis 26 . Mtb -induced lipid peroxidation was decreased 182 

in macrophages upon AKG supplementation as evident by BODIPY C11 staining 183 

(indicator of lipid oxidation) (Figure 5E, F). AKG supplementation also resulted in 184 

increased transcription of GPX4, indicating AKG regulates GPX4 to suppress LPO 185 

during Mtb infection ( Supplemental Figure 2A) . This was concomitant with reduction 186 

in Malondialdehyde  (MDA) levels , oxidative adducts formed as a by -product of lipid 187 

peroxidation , upon AKG supplemented in Mtb -infected macrophages (Supplemental 188 

Figure 2B). Finally, upon quantif ying oxidized vs reduced lipid levels in macrophages 189 

upon Mtb infection , we found an increase in the abundance of reduced lipids upon 190

(Figure 5G, H ).192

DISCUSSION 239 

Tuberculosis accounted for 1.23 million deaths in 2024 alone and as per WHO 240 

report 2025, infections with drug resistant strains of TB are on the rise 56 . The causative 241 

agent, Mycobacterium tuberculosis , a highly adaptive pathogen , continues to 242 

manipula te host cellular pathways to evade immune responses 57 . Mtb mediated 243 

hijacking of host transcriptional responses via regulating epigenetic landscape and 244 

rewiring of host metabolome have garnered much attention in the recent times 4. In our 245 

study , we identify LSD1 as a key metabolic regulator during Mtb infection. 246 

Depending on the site it acts on, LSD1 demethylase activity can lead to gene 247 

repression or activation and has been implicated in regulating various cellular 248 

processes 14 . LSD1 alters cellular metabolic state to support cancer growth and 249 

metastasis 15,31 and hence, LSD1 inhibition as a cancer th erapy is being evaluated with 250 

various inhibitors under clinical trials 19,20,58,59 . We report a pro -pathogen role of LSD1 251 

during Mtb pathogenesis , as LSD1 was induced upon Mtb infection and its knockdown 252 

led to decreased mycobacterial survival . This is in line with p revious research wherein 253 

LSD1 has been implicated in aiding replication of SARS -CoV2 60 and Hepatitis C 254 

virus 61 , modulating host lipid metabolism to support Cryptococcus neoformans 255 

infection 16 . Our question was to discern LSD1 mediated changes induced by Mtb to 256 

support its intracellular survival. 257 

Rewiring of host metabolome as a pathogen strategy to support infection and 258 

persistence has been implicated in various infections 62,63 . In case of Mtb infection ,259 

research has alluded t o diverse metabolic adaptation s including elevated glycolysis 64 ,260 

balanced OXPHOS -Glycolysis 65 as well as suppression of both the pathways 66,67 and 261
