## 摘要
系统性红斑狼疮（SLE 或狼疮）是一种以产生抗核抗体（ANA）和炎症为特征的自身免疫性疾病，尽管 ANA 诱导炎症和组织损伤的机制尚不完全清楚。在此，我们鉴定了由狼疮免疫复合物（IC）驱动的单核吞噬细胞（MP）的独特亚群，包括单核细胞（Mo）和巨噬细胞（MΦ），该复合物由 ANA 及其靶抗原组成。对与 U1-snRNP (snRNP) 狼疮 IC 孵育的人单核细胞进行单细胞转录组测序（scRNA-seq）显示，独特的促炎性单核细胞亚群发生扩增，且炎症基因（包括编码细胞因子、NLRP3 和转录因子的基因）表达上调。蛋白质组学分析表明，这些转录组变化与蛋白质表达强烈相关。单核细胞在响应含有抗 dsDNA 和 Ro60 抗体的其他狼疮 IC 时，也产生了类似的促炎性转录组变化。对狼疮患者皮肤、肾脏和外周血的 scRNA-seq 数据集进行分析，发现存在并扩增了促炎性单核细胞和巨噬细胞群体，其转录组特征与狼疮 IC 刺激的单核细胞中观察到的特征相似。其中一些表达 snRNP IC 基因特征的细胞表现出较低的 I 型干扰素（IFN）特征表达，这表明狼疮 IC 和 I 型 IFN 信号传导可能独立地影响单核细胞亚群。在狼疮性肾炎中，表达 NLRP3 的 CD68+ 巨噬细胞浸润与治疗结果相关。抑制转录因子 ETS2（单核细胞/巨噬细胞驱动炎症的主调节因子）的激活，可减弱狼疮 IC 诱导的促炎性单核细胞激活。总之，这些发现为狼疮 IC 驱动的促炎性单核吞噬细胞在 SLE 发病机制中的作用提供了新见解，并强调了它们作为治疗靶点的相关性。

## Abstract
Systemic lupus erythematosus (SLE or lupus) is an autoimmune disease characterized by anti-nuclear antibody (ANA) production and inflammation, though the mechanisms by which ANAs induce inflammation and tissue injury are incompletely understood. Here, we identified distinct subsets of mononuclear phagocytes (MPs), including monocytes (Mo) and macrophages (M{Phi}), driven by lupus immune complex (IC), comprised of ANAs and their target antigens. scRNA-seq of human Mo incubated with U1-snRNP (snRNP) lupus IC revealed expansion of distinct pro-inflammatory Mo subsets with upregulation of inflammatory genes including those encoding cytokines, NLRP3, and transcription factors. These transcriptomic changes strongly correlated with protein expression, as determined by proteomic analysis. Mo developed similar pro-inflammatory transcriptomic changes in response to other lupus ICs containing anti-dsDNA and Ro60 antibodies. Interrogation of scRNA-seq datasets from the skin, kidney, and peripheral blood of lupus patients revealed the presence and expansion of pro-inflammatory Mo and M{Phi} populations exhibiting transcriptomic signatures similar to those observed in lupus IC-stimulated Mo. Some of these cells expressing the snRNP IC gene signature exhibited low expression of the type I IFN signature, suggesting that lupus IC and type I IFN signaling may independently affect Mo subsets. In lupus nephritis, infiltration of CD68+ M{Phi} expressing NLRP3 was associated with treatment outcomes. Inhibiting activation of the transcription factor ETS2, a master regulator of Mo/M{Phi}-driven inflammation, attenuated lupus IC-induced activation of pro-inflammatory Mo. Collectively, these findings provide novel insights into the role of lupus IC-driven pro-inflammatory MPs in the pathogenesis of SLE and highlight their relevance as therapeutic targets.
