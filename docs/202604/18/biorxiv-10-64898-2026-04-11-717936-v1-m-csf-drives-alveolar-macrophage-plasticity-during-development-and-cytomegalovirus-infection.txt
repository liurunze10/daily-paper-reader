Abstract 31 

Alveolar macrophages (AM) , the most frequent resident immune cells of the lung , are 32 

at the first line of  defence  against respiratory pathogens and instruct structural lung 33 

cells,  e.g.  in tissue repair.  They are long -lived and  receive  their  terminal  phenotypic 34 

imprint  through  signals originating from the  unique location at the tissue -air  interface ,35 

as well as  through  cytokines  like  granulocyte -macrophage colony -stimulating factor 36 

(GM -CSF ) and  transforming  growth  factor -β (TGF -β). However, the  regulatory 37 

mechanisms governing the ir phenotypic  plasticity , which  is conceptually critical for their 38 

positioning  and differentiation  in early life and for their functional adaptation during 39 

infection , remain poorly defined . Here we  explored  respiratory tract  infection with 40 

cytomegalovirus (CMV) , which is closely linked to  mammalian  immune evolution .41 

Co mplementary host -pathogen fate -mapping strategies  revealed  AM  to  constitute  the 42 

bottleneck for efficient  mouse (M) CMV infection.  MCMV infection  induced  macrophage 43 

colony -stimulating factor (M -CSF) in the  alveolar spac e, and  culturing  of AM  in  M-CSF 44 

led  to  a profound remodelling of  morphology,  immuno phenotype, and transcriptional 45 

identity , e.g.  it increased  the  expression of interferon -stimulated genes  (ISG) , which 46 

modulated  susceptibility to infection. Notably,  already at baseline  recently differentiated 47 

neonatal  AM  across specie s retain ed  an M-CSF -associated transcriptional program .48 

This was linked to  reduced permissiveness to respiratory MCMV infection  in vivo .49 

Overall, our findings identif y the role of  M-CSF –dependent signalling  in conferring 50 

plasticity to AM,  when it is most needed,  particularly during early -life establishment and 51 

in response to viral infection. 52

Cytomegaloviruses (CMV)  are ubiquitous  β-herpesviruses that infect their hosts 94 

typically  early  in life . T he lung is a primary infection site, from where the virus 95 

disseminat es  to draining lymph nodes and peripheral organs  (Oduro et al., 2016) . In 96 

immunocompetent individuals, primary infection is typically asymptomatic and leads to 97 

life -long persistence. The virus displays a broad cellular tropism with the ability to infect 98 

fibroblasts, epithelial and endothelial cells, a s well as  immune cell populations. Among 99 

these, myeloid cells have been implicated in early viral replication and dissemination 100 

(Baasch et al., 2020) . In particular, AM have been proposed to represent an initial target 101 

population during respiratory infection  (Baasch et al., 2021; Farrell et al., 2016) .102

contribution of  AM  to early MCMV pathogenesis remain debated . Furthermore, the 104 

cellular  and molecular  factors  that shape the outcome of early  life  CMV infection  of the 105 

respiratory tract  remain incompletely understood .106

Active  human (H)CMV infection and viral replication in monocyte -derived 396

embryogenesis, with additional expression in placental trophoblasts  (Hume et al., 415 

2020) . Although AM ultimately depend on GM -CSF for their differentiation and identity, 416

The difference between the AIC of the constant fit and the AIC of the GAM fit was 1278 

computed. Genes were sorted according to decreasing values of this difference (delta 1279

using as input a distance matrix based on Spearman correlation computed from the 1283 

GAM fitted values. Cluster analysis resulted in 4 clusters of genes  (cluster 1: 414 1284 

genes; cluster 2: 228 genes; cluster 3: 185 genes; cluster 4: 173 genes) . The mean 1285 

trend per cluster was computed from the fitted values of each gene in that cluster. 1286
