Abstract 1

Inflammasome assembly rapidly triggers caspase-1 activation to initiate pyroptosis, 2

an inflammatory cell death characterized by the release of cytosolic contents, including 3

interleukin (IL)-1β/α. Here, we report that inflammasome activation drives necrotic cell death 4

independent of gasdermin D (GSDMD) and GSDME, which are essential executors of 5

pyroptosis by forming a pore on the plasma membrane and increasing membrane permeability. 6

NLRP3 inflammasome activation induced necrotic cell death, coupled with IL-1β/α release in 7

Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– macrophages. Mechanistically, the oligomerization of ninjurin-1 (NINJ1) 8

was caused by inflammasome activation even in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME. 9

Concordantly, glycine, an inhibitor of NINJ1, blocked plasma membrane permeabilization 10 

triggered by inflammasome activation in Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– macrophages, but not in WT 11 

macrophages. The dimerizer-mediated ASC oligomerization promoted NINJ1-mNeonGreen 12 

cluster formation in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME. Moreover, NINJ1 deficiency 13 

prevented membrane permeabilization initiated by ASC oligomerization in Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– 

14 

immortalized bone marrow-derived macrophages (iBMDM). Blocking of phosphatidylserine 15 

(PtdSer) exposure, a feature of inflammasome-driven necrotic cell death, by Xkr8 deficiency 16 

inhibited plasma membrane permeabilization in Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– iBMDM. These results 17 

suggest that inflammasome-triggered activation of caspase-1 itself drives inflammatory 18 

necrotic cell death independent of gasdermins. 19

Introduction 1

Upon multiple stresses, cells undergo regulated cell death (RCD), including 2

apoptosis, pyroptosis, necroptosis, and ferroptosis(Newton et al , 2024). The mode of cell 3

death is a key determinant of multiple biological processes. In particular, whether cell death 4

occurs via apoptosis or other necrotic cell death is critical for initiating inflammatory 5

responses (Newton et al. , 2024). While necrotic cell death is accompanied by the release of 6

inflammatory contents as damage/danger-associated molecular patterns (DAMPs), apoptosis 7

preserves plasma membrane integrity and retains cytosolic content in apoptotic bodies. Since 8

apoptotic bodies can be efficiently removed by efferocytosis, apoptosis is regarded as an anti-9

inflammatory cell death(Doran et al , 2020). Therefore, molecular mechanisms that switch the 10 

mode of cell death are potential targets for inflammatory diseases. 11 

The inflammasome is a molecular complex that functions as a scaffold for caspase-12 

1 activation(Barnett et al , 2023; Karasawa & Takahashi, 2025; Schroder & Tschopp, 2010). 13 

Among inflammasomes, nucleotide-binding oligomerization domain, leucine-rich repeat and 14 

pyrin domain containing 3 (NLRP3) inflammasome, composed of NLRP3, apoptosis-15 

associated speck-like protein containing a caspase recruitment domain (ASC), and caspase-1, 16 

is a key regulator of sterile inflammation because it is activated by various DAMPs, such as 17 

extracellular ATP, monosodium urate (MSU) crystals, and cholesterol crystals. Upon 18 

inflammasome assembly, the active caspase-1 processes precursors of inflammatory cytokines 19 

interleukin (IL)-1β and IL-18 to initiate inflammatory responses. Moreover, the 20 

inflammasome complex triggers the activation of multiple caspases, thereby driving diverse 21 

cell death cascades. The caspase-1-mediated gasdermin D (GSDMD) processing is a major 22 

pathway to initiate pyroptosis, an inflammatory necrotic cell death(Broz et al , 2020; Kayagaki 23 

et al , 2015). Pyroptosis was originally defined as an inflammatory cell death triggered by 24 

inflammatory caspases, including caspase-1, 4, and 5(Brennan & Cookson, 2000; Hagar et al ,25 

2013). Pyroptosis shares several features with apoptosis, such as DNA fragmentation and 26 

phosphatidylserine (PtdSer) exposure. Meanwhile, the rapid induction of increased plasma 27 

membrane permeability represents a hallmark of pyroptosis(Brennan & Cookson, 2000). 28 

Currently, it is generally accepted that the increased plasma membrane permeability during 29 3

pyroptosis is caused by the pore formed by GSDMD. Gasdermin family proteins possess 1

pore-forming activities in their amino-terminal domain (NTD)(Ding et al , 2016). GSDMD-2

NTD processed by caspase-1 binds to inositol phospholipid in the plasma membrane and 3

forms an oligomeric pore that results in increased membrane permeability (Liu et al , 2016). 4

The pore formed by GSDMD plays critical roles in the release of cytosolic content and 5

inflammatory cytokines IL-1β/α and IL-18 to initiate inflammatory responses(Evavold et al ,6

2018; Monteleone et al , 2018). Accumulating evidence suggests that the inflammasome is 7

involved in various inflammatory diseases, including gout, cardiovascular diseases, and 8

neurodegenerative diseases(Barnett et al. , 2023; Takahashi, 2021). Therefore, inflammasome-9

triggered GSDMD processing and induction of pyroptosis are expected as a potent therapeutic 10 

target for various inflammatory diseases. 11 

Previous studies have suggested that inflammasome assembly leads to activation of 12 

caspases other than caspase-1. We have shown that inflammasome activation results in 13 

processing of GSDME via activation of caspase-8/3 in the absence of caspase-1(Aizawa et al ,14 

2020). GSDME, another gasdermin that is processed by caspase-3, functions as an executor 15 

of pyroptosis in a caspase-1-deficient setting. Moreover, multiple studies have suggested that 16 

GSDME is processed during inflammasome or caspase-1 activation in the absence of 17 

GSDMD(Tsuchiya et al , 2019; Zhou & Abbott, 2021). However, it remains unclear whether 18 

the blockade of GSDMD and GSDME suffices for the prevention of pyroptosis. To validate 19 

the role of GSDMD and GSDME in pyroptosis during inflammasome activation, we 20 

established mice lacking both GSDMD and GSDME and found that necrotic cell death occurs 21 

even in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME upon inflammasome activation. 22

2011). We speculated that the exposure of PtdSer is involved in NINJ1 activation because 4

stimulated Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– macrophages. Exposure of PtdSer and entry of SYTOX Deep 8

red occurred in two modes; PtdSer exposure preceded or coincided with plasma membrane 9

permeabilization (Fig. 5A–F, Fig. S4A and B). Furthermore, the increased Annexin V+ 10 

7AAD+ cells during ASC oligomerization were confirmed in DmrBASC-Gsdmd/e DKO 11 

iBMDM (Fig. 5G–I). Of note, Annexin V–7-AAD+ cells were not detected. On the other 12 

hand, cell swelling is a key feature of pyroptosis, and necrotic Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– 

13 

macrophages exhibit swollen morphologies (Fig. 1E). Therefore, we evaluated the volume of 14 

the cell before membrane rupture. Although an increasing trend of cell sphericity was 15

DmrBASC-Gsdmd/e DKO iBMDM (Fig. S4 C–I). Moreover, plasma membrane tension 18

These results indicate that increased cell volume and membrane tension are not the cause of 20

Discussion 15 

Inflammasome activation engages multiple pathways that trigger necrotic cell death 16 

to initiate inflammatory responses. In the present study, we found that combined deficiency of 17 

GSDMD and GSDME failed to prevent necrotic cell death triggered by inflammasome 18 

activation. This necrotic cell death is accompanied by the release of pyroptosis-associated 19 

cytokines, IL-1β and IL-1α. Concordantly, deficiency of GSDMD and GSDME failed to inhibit 20 

infiltration of neutrophils induced by cholesterol crystals in mice. Furthermore, the 21 

inflammasome promotes NINJ1 oligomerization in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME. 22 

Deficiency of NINJ1 prevents not only membrane rupture but also membrane permeabilization 23 

upon inflammasome activation. Among the features associated with inflammasome-driven cell 24 

death, exposure of PtdSer precedes or coincides with plasma membrane permeabilization. The 25 

exposure of PtdSer plays a causative role because the deficiency of Xkr8 attenuated the 26 

exposure of PtdSer and membrane permeabilization during necrotic cell death triggered by 27 

inflammasome activation in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME. Our data clearly suggest that 28 

GSDMD and GSDME are dispensable for the induction of NINJ1-mediated plasma membrane 29 9

Previous studies have suggested that a deficiency of GSDMD is insufficient for the 2

prevention of inflammasome-dependent necrotic cell death(Kayagaki et al. , 2015; Schneider et 3

al , 2017). While caspase-11-initiated pyroptosis is completely blocked by the deficiency of 4

GSDMD, canonical inflammasome-triggered necrotic cell death has not been attributed to a 5

single executor. Zhou et al . reported that GSDME is alternatively processed during NLRP3 6

inflammasome activation in the GSDMD-deficient THP-1 cells(Zhou & Abbott, 2021). The 7

processed GSDME mediates IL-1β release and secondary pyroptosis. However, Broz and his 8

colleagues(Heilig et al , 2020) reported that GSDME has no contribution to AIM2 9

inflammasome-driven necrotic cell death in GSDMD-deficient iBMDM. As previous studies 10 

have investigated the involvement of GSDMD and GSDME in cell lines, we developed mice 11 

lacking GSDMD and GSDME and confirmed that the deficiency of GSDMD and GSDME is 12 

dispensable for the induction of necrotic cell death triggered by the inflammasome in primary 13 

macrophages. 14 

Pyroptosis was originally described as a caspase-1-dependent necrotic cell death in 15 

Salmonella -infected macrophages(Brennan & Cookson, 2000), and had been regarded as a cell 16 

death mediated by inflammatory caspases. Since the identification of GSDMD as a pore-17 

forming protein processed by inflammatory caspases(Kayagaki et al. , 2015; Shi et al , 2015), 18 

pyroptosis has been defined as a gasdermin-dependent cell death (Broz et al. , 2020). In the 19 

present study, in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME, inflammasome activation drives 20 

necrotic cell death that shares similar features with pyroptosis. As Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– 

21 

macrophages released a comparable amount of IL-1β and IL-1α upon NLRP3 inflammasome 22 

activation, we consider that GSDMD determines the kinetics of cell death induction rather than 23 

the secretome of inflammasome-triggered necrotic cell death. Notably, the necrotic cell death 24 

found in this study is dependent on caspase-1. Thus, we postulate that caspase-1 is a critical 25 

determinant of the pyroptotic feature in inflammasome-triggered necrotic cell death. 26 

NINJ1 was reported as an executor of plasma membrane rupture in the final phase 27 

of pyroptosis(Kayagaki et al. , 2021). Subsequent studies have suggested that NINJ1 is involved 28 

in plasma membrane rupture in multiple modes of RCD(Dondelinger et al , 2023; Ramos et al ,29 10

plausible that NINJ1 triggers plasma membrane permeabilization in response to the changes in 7

the condition of the plasma membrane. With regard to the condition of plasma membrane and 8

NINJ1 activation, a recent study demonstrated that NINJ1 is involved in mechanical strain-9

induced plasma membrane rupture(Zhu et al , 2025). However, we did not detect an increase in 10

Necrotic cell death observed in the present study is dependent on caspase-1. 14 

Therefore, we focused on the PtdSer exposure, a well-established feature of caspase-15 

dependent RCD. An asymmetric lipid distribution within the lipid bilayer is regulated by 16 

various enzymes. Flippases and floppases translocate specific lipids from the outer to the 17 

inner leaflet or from the inner to the outer leaflet(Nagata, 2018). Thus, PtdSer is tightly kept 18 

in the inner leaflet of the plasma membrane by ATP-dependent flippases in steady states. In 19 

the present study, we observed that PtdSer exposure precedes or coincides with plasma 20 

membrane permeabilization triggered by the inflammasome in the absence of GSDMD and 21 

GSDME. Among several scramblases involved in PtdSer translocation, Xkr8, a caspase-22 

dependent phospholipid scramblase, was involved in PtdSer exposure and membrane 23 

permeabilization in Gsdmd –/– Gsdme –/– macrophages. Previous studies reported that Ca 2+ -24 

dependent phospholipid scramblase, Ano6 mediates PtdSer exposure in response to GSDMD-25 

pore-dependent Ca 2+ mobilization(Ousingsawat et al , 2018; Yang et al , 2019). However, Ano6 26 

deficiency did not affect PtdSer exposure in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME. Hence, 27 

deficiency of Xkr8 seems to be sufficient to block PtdSer exposure in the GSDMD- and 28 

GSDME-deficient settings. Although further investigations are needed to clarify the link 29 11

that NINJ1 is associated with phospholipid in the outer leaflet of the plasma membrane(Sahoo 2

membrane regulates NINJ1 assembly upon caspase activation. Meanwhile, Xkr8 is activated 4

by cleavage of caspase-3 or caspase-7 and is involved in the exposure of PtdSer during 5

apoptosis. Although we assessed only inflammasome-triggered plasma membrane rupture, it 6

is plausible that Xkr8-mediated PtdSer exposure mediates broad plasma membrane rupture 7

during secondary necrosis. 8

In the present study, although we investigated the mechanisms of necrotic cell death 9

triggered by inflammasome activation in the absence of GSDMD and GSDME, there are 10 

several limitations. First, we investigated the effect of NINJ1-deficiency only in cultured 11 

iBMDM, whose characteristics are distinct from primary macrophages; therefore, further 12 

investigation into the contribution of NINJ1 using NINJ1-deficient mice is required. Second, 13 

although we developed iBMDM expressing NINJ1-mNeonGreen reporter to analyze NINJ1 14 

activation during necrotic cell death, we could not investigate the detailed time course of 15 

NINJ1 activation due to the cell vulnerability to phototoxicity. Therefore, seamless time-lapse 16 

imaging with reduced phototoxicity is required to evaluate Xkr8-mediated PtdSer exposure on 17 

NINJ1 assembly. Further assessment using improved experimental settings will provide 18 

valuable information on the mechanisms of NINJ1 assembly. 19 

In conclusion, we found that inflammasome activation can initiate necrotic cell 20 

death via the gasdermin-independent but NINJ1-dependent mechanism. Our findings will be 21 

valuable for the development of novel therapeutic strategies for inflammasome-associated 22 

inflammatory diseases. 23
